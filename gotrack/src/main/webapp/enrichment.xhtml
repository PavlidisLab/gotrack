The analysis will generate a similarity plot. This plot can compare subsequent editions to each-other (PROXIMAL) or compare each edition to the most current (CURRENT).

The Term Count plot shows counts of unique GO Terms associated with this enrichment analysis over time.

The series shown are:

  • All Terms: Count of unique terms that passed all settings criteria.
  • Significant Terms: Count of unique terms that passed all settings criteria and were deemed significant in the given edition.
  • Rejected Terms: Count of unique terms that did not pass all settings criteria.

The Similarity plot shows metrics that correspond to how similar the sets of significantly enriched terms are over time.

The method for computing similarity is the Jaccard Similarity Index.

The series differ in which sets we are comparing between editions:

  • All Terms: Compare all significant terms.
  • Top 5 Terms: Compare the top 5 terms.
  • Parents of Top Terms: Compare the top 5 terms and all of their ancestors.
  • Genes Backing Top Terms: Compare the genes that are responsible for the top 5 terms.

Clicking on the plot will display the sets corresponding to: Top 5 Terms, Parents of Top Terms, and Genes Backing Top Terms.

The Enrichment tab shows enrichment data for those terms that were significantly enriched at some point in their history. You may change the currently selected edition using the dropdown selector.

Column Definitions:

  • Population: Number of genes in the background (currently all annotated genes) annotated with the given term.
  • Sample: Number of genes in the hitlist annotated with the given term.
  • P-Value: P-Value (after correction if Bonferroni).
  • Rank: Fractional rank of term in the enrichment analysis.

Select a term and Right Click to View more details about the term's enrichment results.

Along the footer of the table is the means to visualize the changes in enrichment results over the history of this hitlist. The inputs allow you to choose from visualizing the Rank or P-Value of the Top N terms or the Selected selected terms.

Note: The Top N plots will include more than N terms, this is because it plots all the terms who have ever been in the top N.
